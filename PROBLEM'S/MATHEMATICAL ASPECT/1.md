# PROBLEM (1):- If we list all the natural numbers below 10 that are multiples of 3 or 5, we get 3, 5, 6 and 9. The sum of these multiples is 23. Find the sum of all the multiples of 3 or 5 below 1000.


## The Mathematical Approach ($O(1)$ Time)
Instead of checking every individual number from 1 to 999, we can use the mathematical formula for the sum of an arithmetic progression.
The Sum of a linear sequence of number is calculated using the formula:

$$\text{Sum} = \frac{n \times (\text{First Term} + \text{Last Term})}{2}$$

## The Inclusion-Exclusion Principle
If we simply add the sum of all multiples of 3 to the sum of all multiples of 5, we will double-count the numbers thatare multiples of both (such as 15, 30, 45, etc.).
To get the correct total, we must use the Inclusion-exclusion priniciple:

$$\text{Total Sum} = \text{Sum(Multiples of 3)} + \text{Sum(Multiples of 5)} - \text{Sum(Multiples of 15)}$$

-----

### Step-by-Step Calculation

#### 1. Multiples of 3 below 1000
* **Sequence:** $3, 6, 9, \dots, 999$
* **Number of terms ($n$):** $999 \div 3 = 333$

$$\text{Sum}_3 = \frac{333 \times (3 + 999)}{2} = \frac{333 \times 1002}{2} = 166,833$$

#### 2. Multiples of 5 below 1000
* **Sequence:** $5, 10, 15, \dots, 995$
* **Number of terms ($n$):** $995 \div 5 = 199$

$$\text{Sum}_5 = \frac{199 \times (5 + 995)}{2} = \frac{199 \times 1000}{2} = 99,500$$

#### 3. Multiples of 15 below 1000
* **Sequence:** $15, 30, 45, \dots, 990$
* **Number of terms ($n$):** $990 \div 15 = 66$

$$\text{Sum}_15 = \frac{66 \times (15 + 990)}{2} = \frac{66 \times 1005}{2} = 33,165$$

-----


### Final Result

$$\text{Total Sum} = 166,833 + 99,500 + 33,165 = \mathbf{233,168}$$
